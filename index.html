Pat Kenny and his 41 years in public affairs

  • Richard Estrada
  • January 21, 2020
  • 0 Comment
  • Uncategorized

Pat Kenny is an unbelievably talented Irish author currently hosting daily radio and television programs. He had a 41-year, high-profile working life with RTÉ in which he was their greatest-earning host for many years.

Kenny was a television presenter on RTÉ’s Today Now, a mid-1980s public relations show. In 1988, having taken on the role of co-presenter of the Eurovision Song Contest, he began a rare course for a multitude of public relations activities.

His Toy Show interview with the actor Jerry Seinfeld while managing The Late Late Show was among the highlights of Kenny’s career. Seinfeld seemed straight-lipped, even as Pat Kenny had played Jerry Sein-field. Kenny revealed on 27 March 2009 that he’ll leave at the end of the season as host of The Late Late Show.

The TV and radio host is setting up a large-scale profit for himself and his family The Newstalk host has just released a succinct results report of his business, Pat Jenny media services, with an annual income of € 1,526.884.

Newstalk host has been an on-air staple for the last four decades since anchoring some of the highest-rated shows on our televisions, including The Late Late Show, which he hosted for ten years. In 2013, Kenny left RTE and took a stance with Newstalk Fm. Newstalk recently poached him into a € 2 m deal with the Dalkey-based radio network.

He has signed an elaborate contract with Virgin Media for his best-known public relations series, in which he addresses the issues facing the nation. It is known that he has signed a deal with TV3 for the weekly series ahead of him.
